# Voice Guidelines — Blog Rewrite

## Core Principle
Sound like a peer creator talking to another creator over coffee, not a SaaS company writing documentation.

## Before/After Examples

**B2B SaaS tone (before):**
> "The administrative overhead of manually uploading each video to the appropriate playlist represents a significant cognitive cost for content creators managing multiple channels or series."

**Creator peer tone (after):**
> "Uploading every video one by one to the right playlist? That busywork adds up fast — especially when you're juggling multiple channels or series."

---

**B2B SaaS tone (before):**
> "This approach offers an asymmetric advantage in the current competitive landscape, enabling creators to allocate their finite temporal resources toward content ideation and production."

**Creator peer tone (after):**
> "This is your cheat code. Stop burning time on boring stuff and get back to making the content that actually matters."

---

**B2B SaaS tone (before):**
> "In the following sections, we will examine the key features that distinguish our solution from native YouTube Studio, with a particular emphasis on..."

**Creator peer tone (after):**
> "Here's what makes this different from YouTube Studio — and why you'll actually want to use it."

---

## Specific Rules

### 1. Kill the jargon
- "administrative overhead" → "busywork" / "manual grunt work"
- "cognitive cost" / "cognitive load" → "mental drain" / "brain space"
- "context switching tax" → "stopping and starting"
- "asymmetric advantage" / "competitive landscape" → "cheat code" / "edge" / just say what it is
- "finite temporal resources" → "time"
- "content ideation and production" → "making content" / "creating"
- "pipeline" / "workflow" (as nouns everywhere) → be specific: "upload process" / "editing setup" / "how you work"
- "leverage" → use / tap into / just say it directly
- "utilize" → "use"
- "facilitate" → "help" / "let you"
- "optimize" / "optimization" → "make better" / "improve" / "streamline"
- "ecosystem" → "setup" / "tools" / nothing
- "solution" → "tool" / "app" / "thing"
- "production-grade" → just delete this
- "robust" → "solid" / "powerful" / nothing
- "seamless" → "smooth" / nothing (show don't tell)
- "streamline" → "speed up" / "simplify" / nothing

### 2. Short sentences
- Break every sentence over 25 words into two or three.
- One idea per sentence. If there are two ideas, use two sentences.
- Vary sentence length for rhythm: short, punchy. Then occasional longer one.

### 3. No throat-clearing
Delete these phrases entirely:
- "Let's take a closer look at"
- "In this post, we'll explore"
- "It's worth noting that"
- "It goes without saying that"
- "At the end of the day"
- "In the world of content creation"
- "As a content creator"
- "When it comes to"
- "In order to"
- "The fact of the matter is"
- "It is important to"
- "One of the key things to understand is"

Just start with the actual content. If you need a transition, use a short one: "Here's the thing:" or "So here's why that matters:" or just a blank line.

### 4. Use contractions
- "you will" → "you'll"
- "you are" → "you're"
- "it is" → "it's"
- "do not" → "don't"
- "cannot" → "can't"
- "will not" → "won't"
- "that is" → "that's"
- "there is" → "there's"
- "we are" → "we're"
- "we have" → "we've"
- "I am" → "I'm"
- "I have" → "I've"
- "I will" → "I'll"

### 5. No passive voice
- "It can be done by" → "You can do it by"
- "The file is uploaded when" → "The file uploads when"
- "Mistakes are made" → "You'll make mistakes" (and that's fine)

### 6. Add personality
- Opinions are allowed. Say "this is overrated" or "this is actually useful" or "honestly, this changed everything."
- Direct address: "you" / "your" throughout
- Rhetorical questions: "Sound familiar?" / "Been there?" / "What actually happens?"
- Occasional emphasis: italics for emphasis, bold for key points, dashes for punch
- Hot takes: don't be afraid to say something is bad or good

### 7. Be specific, not abstract
- "The combined effect of these optimizations results in" → "Put it all together and you get"
- "Research indicates that creators who implement" → "Creators who use"
- "Data suggests" → just say what the data says or delete it

### 8. Structure
- H2 headings: short, punchy, conversational. Not "Understanding the Upload Process" but "How Uploading Actually Works" or "The Boring Part"
- H3 headings: even shorter. Sometimes just 2-3 words.
- Bullet lists with 1-2 word intros if helpful
- **Bold** key takeaways
- Use `>` for emphasis or key quotes

### 9. Keep what works
- Technical accuracy
- Specific numbers, data, stats
- YouTube-specific terminology and platform features
- Internal links between posts
- All advice, steps, how-tos
- Category-specific examples (gamers, educators, fitness coaches, etc.)

### 10. What to NEVER change
- The BlogPost export structure
- The `slug`, `title`, `description`, `date`, `category`, `readingTime`, `published` fields
- Internal cross-links like `[text](/blog/other-post)`
- import and export statements
- The file name and exported const name
